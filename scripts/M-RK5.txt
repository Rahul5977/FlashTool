[CLIP 2 — 8s | THE PATTERN — Part 1]
Tone: Flat, factual. This is not a one-off — it's every day.
The "boss puchta hai" line needs a pause after — let the silence land.
 
"Roz yahi hota hai.
Sab bolte hain — main chup rehta hoon.
Boss puchta hai kuch add karna?"
 
 
[CLIP 3 — 8s | THE PATTERN — Part 2]
Tone: The most painful beat. Slight edge of bitterness on "credit usko."
"Jaise dikhta hi nahi" — quiet, not dramatic.
 
"Main bolta no sir, sab theek.
Phir wahi idea koi aur bolta hai.
Credit usko milta hai."
 
 
[CLIP 4 — 8s | THE TURN]
Tone: First shift. He found someone who actually listened.
"Tera idea tha?" — he says it like Rishika said it. Simple. Direct.
 
"Ek raat Rishika se bola —
yaar, office mein kuch bol nahi paata.
Usne pucha — tera idea tha?"
 
 
[CLIP 5 — 8s | RISHIKA'S LINE — QUOTED BY GUY]
⚠️ Rishika NEVER on screen.
Guy quotes her words — slowly, like he's still processing the simplicity of it.
"Mera ek point hai" — he says these 4 words separately. That's the whole trick.
 
"Usne bola — yaar, kal meeting mein
sirf ek sentence bol.
Mera ek point hai. Bas itna kaafi hai."
 
 
[CLIP 6 — 8s | PAYOFF]
Tone: Small win. Not triumphant — quietly surprised it worked.
"Colleague bola — yaar accha point tha" = the outside validation that makes it real.
Small genuine smile. Earned, not performed.
 
"Bol diya. Sir, mera ek point hai.
Meeting ke baad colleague bola —
yaar, aaj accha point tha."
