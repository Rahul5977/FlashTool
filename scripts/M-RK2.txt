- Hook: recounting with a slightly self-deprecating smile — "andar se bol
  raha tha" should feel funny and real, not sad. Everyone has been there.
- Pattern: flat, factual. "Main hoon, log hain, aur main bas... chup."
  The pause before "chup" matters — that's where the viewer feels it.
- Rishika's trick: he says it like he's slightly skeptical it'll work,
  but repeating it anyway. Not excited. Curious.
- Payoff: small genuine smile. Not a grin — a "wait, that actually worked"
  expression. The win is tiny and that's exactly why it's believable.
- NO enthusiasm anywhere. Real introverts don't suddenly become extroverts.
  One conversation started = one real win.

1. INTERNAL VOICEOVER → SPOKEN TO CAMERA
   "Kuch bol, kuch bhi bol" was marked as internal voiceover —
   Veo cannot render thoughts. Now the Guy recounts it directly to
   camera. Same energy, same cringe-recognition, speakable on screen.

2. TEXT OVERLAY SEQUENCE → ONE SPOKEN LINE
   "Lift mein chup, canteen mein chup..." was a rapid text-overlay
   sequence — a reel editing technique Veo cannot generate.
   Compressed into one clean spoken line that covers the same feeling.

3. SECOND CHARACTER (chai area person) → REMOVED
   The other person walking away, smiling, speaking, walking together
   required a second AI-generated face with no reference = hallucination.
   Now the Guy recounts what the person said — viewer imagines them.
   This is actually MORE believable. Every real testimonial is one person.

4. RISHIKA ON SCREEN → QUOTED BY GUY
   Same words, delivered by the Guy: "Rishika ne ek cheez boli —"
   then her exact trick. Zero second character. Zero face switch.
   The emotional value of her advice is completely preserved.

5. DURATION: 26s → ~29s
   26s = 3×8s + 2s last clip (under minimum 5s). Restructured to
   3×8s + 1×5s = 29s. App handles this with custom clip durations.
